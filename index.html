Gap Analysis

Modal → Sign-up (P0)

Current: 66.60% conversion | Target: 80% | Gap: 13.40 percentage points

3,672 users lost

Possible reasons: Sign-up friction (too many fields, no social login), privacy concerns, users wanted to explore more before committing.

Recommended solutions: Add Google/Apple one-tap sign-up. Reduce form to email-only with progressive profiling. Show "your game idea is saved" messaging to motivate completion.

What-if: Improving from 66.60% to 80% would yield ~1,473 additional sign-ups, resulting in ~985 more Wish1 submissions per month (at 98.66% post-signup conversion).

AI Response → Modal (P1)

Current: 73.44% conversion | Target: 85% | Gap: 11.56 percentage points

3,978 users lost

Possible reasons: Users get AI brainstorm value but leave without being prompted to sign up. The modal trigger may fire too late, or the AI response quality doesn't compel action.

Recommended solutions: Trigger the sign-up modal earlier (after first substantive AI response). Add inline CTAs within AI responses. Show a preview of what the game could look like.

What-if: Improving from 73.44% to 85% would yield ~1,730 additional modal views, cascading to ~1,128 more Wish1s.

Session → Message (P2)

Current: 86.65% conversion | Target: 92% | Gap: 5.35 percentage points

2,464 users lost

Possible reasons: Users see the brainstorm interface but don't know what to type. The input field may not be prominent enough. Some users may be evaluating the product without intent to engage.

Recommended solutions: Add suggested prompts ("Try: Make a space shooter"). Auto-focus the input field. Show example game ideas as inspiration tiles.

What-if: Improving from 86.65% to 92% would yield ~989 more messages, cascading to ~485 more Wish1s.

Retention Outlook

Wish1 → Wish10: 9.15% retention

Of 7,223 users who submit Wish1, 3,890 make it to Wish2 (53.86%), 1,513 reach Wish5 (38.89% from W2), and 661 hit Wish10 (43.69% from W5). Focus the retention effort on the first-to-second wish transition.

Syria (1,334 PV, 0% E2E), Turkey (963 PV, 0% E2E), Bangladesh (758 PV, 0% E2E), and Nigeria (506 PV, 0% E2E) collectively send 3,561 page views with zero wish_10 completions. Syria specifically drops to zero at signup -- all 109 users who reach the sign-up modal fail to complete. This pattern is consistent with bot farms or click fraud originating from these regions.

SIGNAL 3

App Webview Traffic (Referrer Stripped)

When users click links in apps like Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, WhatsApp, or Telegram, the app opens the page in a built-in webview. These webviews strip the HTTP referrer header, causing Google Analytics to classify the traffic as "(none)/direct." This explains: (1) why it is 85% mobile/79% Android, (2) why Android sign-up is only 10.03% -- OAuth popups often fail in webviews, and (3) why the AI → Create rate is only 28.82% -- webview users may be casually browsing shared links, not intending to create.

SIGNAL 4

Low-Intent Browsing

High PV from South Asia and MENA regions (10,229 PV combined) with extremely low conversion (0.16% E2E) suggests users who encounter Astrocade through shared links or social media but have no genuine intent to create a game. They browse briefly and leave.

Impact & Recommendations

Impact: Filtering (none)/Direct Reveals True Performance

Current non-ads E2E: 1.75% (575 wish_10 from 32,896 PV)

Without (none)/direct: 4.69% E2E (476 wish_10 from ~10,158 organic search PV)

E2E jumps from 1.75% to 4.69%

Removing this single traffic source from the calculation shows that the organic non-ads funnel actually outperforms paid ads (3.51% E2E). The (none)/direct traffic is not a funnel problem to fix -- it is a measurement problem to filter.

Recommendation 1: Implement Bot Detection / CAPTCHA

Add bot detection (e.g., reCAPTCHA v3, Cloudflare Bot Management) before the sign-up step. This would filter out automated traffic from zero-conversion regions (Syria, Turkey, Bangladesh, Nigeria) and "Other" device categories. Expected impact: cleaner funnel data and reduced server costs from bot traffic.

Recommendation 2: Fix OAuth/Sign-up Flow on Android Webviews

The 10.03% Android Modal → Signup rate (vs 66.76% on Windows) strongly suggests OAuth sign-up breaks in app webviews. Investigate: (1) Does Google/Apple OAuth popup get blocked in Facebook/Instagram/TikTok webviews? (2) Can you implement in-page sign-up (email + password) as a fallback when OAuth is unavailable? If even 30% of these webview users could sign up, that would add ~355 additional sign-ups per month.

Recommendation 3: Filter (none)/Direct from Funnel Metrics

Create a separate "quality traffic" dashboard that excludes (none)/direct, Google Display, and Taboola. Use this filtered view as the primary decision-making metric. The current blended non-ads E2E of 1.75% is misleading and could lead to under-investment in organic search, which actually performs at 4.69% E2E.

Recommendation 4: Add UTM Parameters to All Shared Links

Prevent future (none) classification by ensuring all links shared on social media, messaging apps, email campaigns, and partner sites include UTM parameters (utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign). This would properly attribute webview traffic to its actual source (e.g., Facebook, WhatsApp, Telegram) and allow targeted optimization. Consider implementing a link shortener with automatic UTM tagging.
