1

Funnel Structure Definition

The funnel consists of 10 sequential steps, from initial page view on /create through the brainstorm experience, sign-up conversion, and wish-based retention milestones. Data sourced from 2 Google Search Ad campaigns running in February 2026 (Feb 1–28). The brainstorm_ai_response step was included to capture AI system reliability as a distinct conversion point.

Bottleneck Zone Identification

Three bottleneck zones identified based on where the funnel narrows most sharply:

Zone A — Conversion Gauntlet (AI Response → Sign-up): Combined pass-through = 73.43% × 66.60% = 48.90%. Nearly half of engaged users are lost in these two steps.

Zone B — Wish Retention Collapse (Wish1 → wish10): Only 661 / 7,223 = 9.15% of first-wish creators reach 10 wishes. Three consecutive low-conversion steps compound to massive attrition.

Zone C — Session → Message (13.35% drop): 2,464 users start a brainstorm but never type a message. A meaningful but secondary loss point.
5

Positive Signal Identification

Steps with conversion rates above 90% are flagged as healthy:

• Page → Session: 97.95% — excellent landing page engagement
• Message → AI Response: 93.63% — reliable AI system performance
• SignUp → Wish1: 98.66% — near-perfect handoff to creation

The E2E rate of 3.51% is strong for paid search acquisition funnels with this many steps.

• Conversion rates remain relatively stable day-to-day, suggesting structural rather than temporal issues

Bottlenecks Deep Dive

Bottleneck 1: AI Response → Sign-up (The Conversion Gauntlet)

48.90%
Combined pass-through rate
7,650
Total users lost (3,978 + 3,672)

The two-step sequence from AI Response → Sign-up Modal → Sign-up Complete has a combined pass-through of just 73.43% × 66.60% = 48.90%. Of 14,971 users who receive an AI response, only 7,321 complete sign-up. This is the biggest absolute-volume loss zone in the funnel.

AI Response → Modal (26.57% drop): 3,978 users engage with the AI but leave before seeing the sign-up modal. The 26-second median gap suggests users are reading the AI response but not finding enough motivation to continue. The modal trigger may come too late, or the AI response may not clearly communicate next steps.

Modal → Sign-up (33.40% drop): 3,672 users see the modal but don't complete. The 25-second median decision time indicates a snap judgment — users quickly evaluate the sign-up form and leave. This points to UX friction: too many fields, missing social auth options, or insufficient trust signals.

Bottleneck 2: Wish Retention (Wish1 → wish10)

9.15%
Wish1 → wish10 survival rate
6,562
Users lost between Wish1 and wish10

Of 7,223 users who create their first wish, only 661 reach 10 wishes — a 9.15% survival rate. The three retention steps compound catastrophically:

Wish1 → Wish2 (53.86%): 3,333 users create one game and never return for a second. The 8m 52s median gap suggests users leave after seeing their first game result. If the result doesn't impress, there's no hook to bring them back.

Wish2 → wish5 (38.89%): The steepest relative drop. 2,377 users create 2 games but stop before 5. The 13m 52s gap shows engagement tails off quickly.

wish5 → wish10 (43.69%): 852 users who reach 5 wishes don't make it to 10. By this point, users have invested ~45 minutes. Those who leave may have exhausted their ideas or hit quality ceilings.

Combined: 53.86% × 38.89% × 43.69% = 9.15%. This is the #1 growth lever for the business.

Findings & Recommendations

Key Findings

1 The funnel has a clear "conversion gauntlet" between AI Response and Sign-up.

7,650 users are lost across these two steps (3,978 + 3,672). The combined pass-through rate is only 48.90%, making this the highest-volume loss zone.

2 Wish retention is the #1 growth lever.

Only 9.15% of first-wish creators reach wish_10. The compounding effect of three sub-55% steps creates massive attrition. 5,710 users create one game and stop.

3 Top-of-funnel is highly efficient.

97.95% page-to-session rate and 93.63% message-to-AI rate show the brainstorm experience works well technically. Users engage quickly (18 seconds to AI response).

4 Sign-up to first wish is near-perfect (98.66%).

Once users commit to sign-up, they immediately create. This validates the product's core value proposition at the point of activation.

5 The 25-second sign-up decision window is critical.

Users make a snap judgment about sign-up. Every unnecessary form field or missing trust signal in this 25-second window costs conversions at a 33.40% drop rate.

6 The first game result is a make-or-break moment.

46.14% of users stop after one wish. The 8m 52s median gap before wish 2 suggests users evaluate their first game and decide not to continue. Game quality or lack of guidance may be the cause.

7 Daily volume varies 2x but conversion rates are stable.

Page views range from 426 (Feb 17) to 940 (Feb 10), but E2E conversion stays relatively consistent, confirming issues are structural, not temporal.

8 3.51% E2E conversion is a strong baseline for paid search.

For a 10-step funnel driven by paid search, converting 3.51% of ad clicks to 10-wish creators is solid. The improvements identified could push this above 6%.

Recommendations

P0

Improve First Game Experience to Drive Wish Retention

Address the 46.14% drop after first wish. Ensure the first game result is high-quality and delightful. Add post-creation prompts like "Try making a puzzle game next" or "See what happens with a different theme." Provide visible progress indicators (e.g., "2 of 10 wishes used") to motivate continuation.

P0

Add Guided Wish Suggestions Between Games

Users who create 2 games but stop before 5 (61.11% drop) likely run out of ideas. Implement AI-powered wish suggestions based on their previous creations. Show a "creators like you also made..." feed. Reduce the cognitive load of coming up with new game ideas.

P1

Simplify Sign-up Flow

Reduce the 33.40% sign-up drop by minimizing form fields. Add Google/Apple social auth to enable one-tap sign-up. Consider progressive profiling (collect email only, gather details later). The 25-second decision window demands a frictionless form.

P1

Optimize Sign-up Modal Trigger Timing

3,978 users get an AI response but never see the modal. Test triggering the modal earlier (e.g., immediately after the AI response loads) or adding a compelling CTA within the AI response itself. The 26-second gap may indicate users read the response and leave before the modal appears.

P2

Reduce Session → Message Drop

13.35% of users start a brainstorm session but never type a message. Add auto-populated prompt suggestions or a "click to start" template to reduce blank-page syndrome. Consider auto-focusing the input field with a pulsing cursor.

P2

Implement Re-engagement for Wish Dropoffs

For users who created 1–4 wishes and stopped, implement email/push re-engagement within 24 hours. Highlight new features, show what their games look like with updates, or offer time-limited incentives. The ~9-minute gap between wishes suggests these are same-session decisions — re-engagement needs to happen quickly.

Findings & Solutions

Key Findings

1 Retention is the #1 growth lever — fixing it doubles output.

10,449 users create their first game. Only 1,137 reach 10 (10.88%). Improving retention to 20% would yield ~2,090 wish_10 — nearly doubling output without spending more on acquisition.

2 The first game is the most important moment in the entire funnel.

4,720 users (45.17%) stop after their first wish. The 8m 52s median gap before wish 2 suggests users evaluate their first game result and decide it’s not worth continuing. First-game quality is the single highest-leverage product investment.

3 The sign-up gauntlet loses 12,054 users across two steps.

AI→Modal (28.10% drop) + Modal→SignUp (34.88% drop) = combined 46.10% pass-through. These are users who brainstormed, typed a message, and got an AI response. They’re engaged. The sign-up wall and modal timing are blocking conversion.

4 The top of funnel is already efficient — don’t over-optimize it.

PV→Session (97.95%) and Message→AI (94.15%) are near-perfect. Session→Message (84.77%) has room for improvement but is not the priority. Focus resources on conversion and retention.

5 Improving Wish2→Wish5 from 41.82% to 55% adds ~310 wish_10 (+27%).

This is the leakiest single step. Users who make 2 games have shown real interest. Getting them to 5 is about guidance, inspiration, and game quality — not acquisition.
