Gap Analysis

Modal → Sign-up (P0)

Current: 66.60% conversion | Target: 80% | Gap: 13.40 percentage points

3,672 users lost

Possible reasons: Sign-up friction (too many fields, no social login), privacy concerns, users wanted to explore more before committing.

Recommended solutions: Add Google/Apple one-tap sign-up. Reduce form to email-only with progressive profiling. Show "your game idea is saved" messaging to motivate completion.

What-if: Improving from 66.60% to 80% would yield ~1,473 additional sign-ups, resulting in ~985 more Wish1 submissions per month (at 98.66% post-signup conversion).

AI Response → Modal (P1)

Current: 73.44% conversion | Target: 85% | Gap: 11.56 percentage points

3,978 users lost

Possible reasons: Users get AI brainstorm value but leave without being prompted to sign up. The modal trigger may fire too late, or the AI response quality doesn't compel action.

Recommended solutions: Trigger the sign-up modal earlier (after first substantive AI response). Add inline CTAs within AI responses. Show a preview of what the game could look like.

What-if: Improving from 73.44% to 85% would yield ~1,730 additional modal views, cascading to ~1,128 more Wish1s.

Session → Message (P2)

Current: 86.65% conversion | Target: 92% | Gap: 5.35 percentage points

2,464 users lost

Possible reasons: Users see the brainstorm interface but don't know what to type. The input field may not be prominent enough. Some users may be evaluating the product without intent to engage.

Recommended solutions: Add suggested prompts ("Try: Make a space shooter"). Auto-focus the input field. Show example game ideas as inspiration tiles.

What-if: Improving from 86.65% to 92% would yield ~989 more messages, cascading to ~485 more Wish1s.

Retention Outlook

Wish1 → Wish10: 9.15% retention

Of 7,223 users who submit Wish1, 3,890 make it to Wish2 (53.86%), 1,513 reach Wish5 (38.89% from W2), and 661 hit Wish10 (43.69% from W5). The steepest retention cliff is Wish1 → Wish2, where nearly half of new users churn after their first wish. Median time from Wish1 to Wish2 is 832s (~14 min), and Wish2 to Wish5 is 1,363s (~23 min). Focus the retention effort on the first-to-second wish transition.

Gap Analysis

Modal → Sign-up (P0)

Current: 49.43% conversion | Target: 66.60% (match ads) | Gap: 17.17 percentage points

4,186 users lost

Possible reasons: Non-ads users have lower commitment than ad-clickers. The sign-up wall appears before enough value is demonstrated. No social login or one-tap options.

Recommended solutions: Implement Google/Apple one-tap sign-up. Let users see a partial game preview before requiring sign-up. A/B test delaying the modal by one more AI interaction.

What-if: Matching ads-level conversion (66.60%) would yield ~1,421 additional sign-ups and ~1,389 more Wish1s per month.

AI Response → Modal (P1)

Current: 53.97% conversion | Target: 73.44% (match ads) | Gap: 19.47 percentage points

7,060 users lost

Possible reasons: Non-ads users may be more exploratory and leave after getting AI brainstorm value. The modal trigger timing may not account for different browsing behaviors.

Recommended solutions: Trigger the modal more aggressively for organic users. Add "save your game idea" urgency messaging. Show social proof of games created.

What-if: Matching ads-level conversion (73.44%) would yield ~2,986 more modal views, cascading to ~1,446 more Wish1s.

Session → Message (P2 -- inflated by junk)

Current: 65.01% conversion | Ads benchmark: 86.65% | Gap: 21.64 percentage points

9,554 users lost

Possible reasons: This metric is heavily inflated by (none)/direct junk traffic (54.5% of all non-ads PV). Organic search users likely engage at rates closer to ads. Filtering junk would give a more accurate picture.

Recommended solutions: Block or filter junk traffic sources. For legitimate users, add suggested prompt tiles and auto-focus the input field.

What-if: If organic search users match the ads rate (86.65%), significantly more users would enter the conversion funnel.
